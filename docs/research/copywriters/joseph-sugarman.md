# Joseph Sugarman Copywriting Research

> **Purpose**: Reference document for creating a specialist copywriting agent for home improvement/listing prep website (Prep It List It Sell It Services).

## Overview

Joseph Sugarman (1938-2022) was a legendary direct response copywriter who founded JS&A Group, Inc. and sold over 20 million pairs of BluBlocker sunglasses. He pioneered many techniques that are now standard in direct marketing, including toll-free phone orders with credit cards and infomercial-style advertising.

**Key Works**:
- "The Adweek Copywriting Handbook" (also known as "Advertising Secrets of the Written Word")
- "Triggers: 30 Sales Tools You Can Use to Control the Mind of Your Prospect"

---

## Core Framework

Sugarman's copywriting system consists of:
- **17 Axioms** (fundamental principles)
- **10 Graphic Elements** (visual components of an ad)
- **23 Copy Elements** (written components)
- **31 Psychological Triggers** (persuasion mechanisms)

## The Slippery Slide Concept

Sugarman's most famous technique: making copy so compelling that readers cannot stop reading.

### The Core Principle

> "Your readers should be so compelled to read your copy that they cannot stop reading until they read all of it as if sliding down a slippery slide."

### How It Works

1. **Headline** gets attention and draws reader to first sentence
2. **First sentence** compels reading of second sentence
3. **Each element** exists only to move reader to the next
4. **Momentum builds** until reader reaches the call to action
5. **Reader takes action** of their own accord (not pushed)

### Technique: Keep First Sentences SHORT

Sugarman believed short first sentences create less resistance:

**Examples**:
- "It's easy."
- "It had to happen."
- "Picture this."
- "This is the truth."

**Why it works**: Easier to read = fewer obstacles = reader gets "sucked in"

### Home Improvement Application

| Generic Opening | Sugarman-Style Opening |
|----------------|------------------------|
| "Our company has been providing quality home improvement services for over 10 years..." | "It was 2 AM." |
| "Are you looking to sell your home and want to maximize value?" | "She called in a panic." |
| "We offer comprehensive listing prep services..." | "Three days. That's all they had." |

## Seeds of Curiosity

Transition phrases that "grease" the slippery slide and keep readers moving.

### Examples

- "But there's more."
- "So read on."
- "Let me explain."
- "But I didn't stop there."
- "Here's why."
- "Now here's the interesting part."

### Usage Guidelines

- Place at end of paragraphs
- Use sparingly (like manure: a little greens the grass, too much stinks up the town)
- Create curiosity loops that demand resolution

### Home Improvement Application

> "We finished the kitchen in record time. But that's not what impressed her."

> "The realtor predicted a $15,000 bump in asking price. She was wrong."

## Creating the Buying Environment

The complete experience surrounding your message shapes buyer psychology.

### Key Principle

> "First we want the reader to read the copy... Then we create the type of environment through copy that causes the prospect to feel comfortable in exchanging his or her hard-earned money for your product or service."

### Environment Elements

| Element | High-End Feeling | Urgent/Sale Feeling |
|---------|------------------|---------------------|
| Colors | Black, gold, silver | Red, yellow |
| Typography | Elegant, serif | Bold, sans-serif |
| Layout | Spacious, clean | Dense, busy |
| Language | Sophisticated | Direct, urgent |

### First Paragraphs Purpose

The first few paragraphs must:
1. Create a welcoming atmosphere
2. Build rapport with the reader
3. Establish the foundation for persuasion
4. Set expectations for what follows

### Home Improvement Application

For Prep It (premium positioning):
- Use clean, professional layouts
- Navy and gold palette (per brand)
- Conversational but professional tone
- Open with relatable homeowner scenarios

## The 17 Copywriting Axioms

Sugarman's fundamental principles of effective advertising copy:

### Axiom 1: Copywriting as Mental Process
Copywriting reflects your experiences, knowledge, and ability to process information for selling.

### Axiom 2: All Elements Serve One Purpose
Every element exists to get the reader to read the first sentence.

### Axiom 3: First Sentence Purpose
The sole purpose of the first sentence is to get you to read the second sentence.

### Axiom 4: Create Buying Environment
Your layout and first paragraphs must create the buying environment most conducive to the sale.

### Axiom 5: Get Reader Agreement
Get the reader to say "yes" and harmonize with accurate, truthful statements.

### Axiom 6: The Slippery Slide
Readers should be so compelled they cannot stop reading.

### Axiom 7: Don't Assume Constraints
When solving problems, don't assume constraints that aren't really there.

### Axiom 8: Power of Curiosity
Keep copy interesting through curiosity.

### Axiom 9: Sell Concepts, Not Products
Never sell a product or service. Always sell a concept.

### Axiom 10: Incubation Process
Your subconscious uses all knowledge/experience to solve problems; efficiency is dictated by time, environment, and creative orientation.

### Axiom 11: Copy Length
Copy should be long enough to cause the reader to take the action you request.

### Axiom 12: Personal Communication
Every communication should be personal, regardless of medium.

### Axiom 13: Logical Flow
Ideas should flow logically, anticipating and answering prospect questions.

### Axiom 14: Editing is Refinement
In editing, express exactly what you want with the fewest words.

### Axiom 15: Sell the Cure
Selling a cure is easier than selling prevention.

### Axiom 16: Mental Engagement
Keep your audience thinking; obvious copy bores readers.

### Axiom 17: Storytelling Power
Telling a story can effectively sell, create environment, and build emotional bonds.

## The 24 Psychological Triggers

Sugarman identified these buying motivators:

### Trust & Credibility Triggers
1. **Honesty** - Admit flaws upfront; share "dirty laundry"
2. **Integrity** - Be consistent in word and action
3. **Credibility** - Be believable; resolve all objections

### Value Triggers
4. **Value and Proof of Value** - Compare to competitors
5. **Justify the Purchase** - Give reasons ("You deserve it")
6. **Greed** - Appeal to desire for more/better

### Authority Triggers
7. **Establish Authority** - Demonstrate expertise
8. **Satisfaction Conviction** - Show confidence in product

### Product Triggers
9. **Nature of Product** - Match message to product type
10. **Current Fads** - Tap into what's trending
11. **Timing** - Right message at right time

### Social Triggers
12. **Desire to Belong** - "Join others like you"
13. **Desire to Collect** - Completeness motivation
14. **Human Relationships** - Connection and relatedness

### Psychological Triggers
15. **Curiosity** - Create knowledge gaps
16. **Sense of Urgency** - Time/quantity limits
17. **Instant Gratification** - Immediate benefits
18. **Exclusivity/Rarity/Uniqueness** - Special access
19. **Simplicity** - Easy to understand and act
20. **Guilt** - Responsibility and obligation
21. **Specificity** - Precise details build credibility
22. **Familiarity** - Known and comfortable
23. **Hope** - Future possibilities
24. **Involvement/Ownership** - Mental possession

### Home Improvement Application

| Trigger | Application for Prep It |
|---------|------------------------|
| Honesty | "We won't sugar-coat it: some homes need more work than others" |
| Proof of Value | "Homes we prepare sell for 3-5% above asking (vs. 2% for DIY prep)" |
| Urgency | "Your listing date is set. Every day counts." |
| Involvement | "Walk through your home like a buyer would" |
| Specificity | "We completed 47 kitchen updates in Santa Clarita this year" |

## Objection Handling

### Core Principle

> "If you feel that your prospect might raise some objection when you are describing a product, then raise the objection yourself."

### Two Approaches

1. **Ridicule**: Make fun of the objection (Avis: "We're No.2, so we try harder")
2. **Resolve**: Mention objection and explain why it isn't a problem

### Why It Works

- Ignoring objections = ignoring the consumer
- Consumers are too sharp and will not buy if concerns are unaddressed
- Addressing objections builds trust

### Sugarman's Approach

He frequently "aired the dirty laundry" of products upfront:
- Sold ugly thermostats by acknowledging they looked bad
- Addressed BluBlocker concerns before customers could raise them

### Home Improvement Application

| Common Objection | Sugarman-Style Response |
|-----------------|-------------------------|
| "This will cost too much" | "Yes, quality prep work isn't cheap. But here's what cheap costs you at closing..." |
| "I can do this myself" | "You absolutely could. Most homeowners spend 47 hours on prep. Let's talk about what your time is worth." |
| "I don't trust contractors" | "You've been burned before. We get it. That's why we [specific trust mechanism]." |

## Making Technical Products Exciting

Sugarman sold calculators, digital watches, thermostats, and computers through compelling copy.

### The Research Approach

1. **Become obsessed** with the product
2. **Know more than the manufacturer** about it
3. **Find the unexpected angle** (often a "lesser known" feature)
4. **Lead with that differentiator**

### Case Study: The Thermostat

**Product**: Magic Stat thermostat (not visually appealing)

**Research Discovery**: The biggest pain point wasn't energy savings - it was installation difficulty requiring expensive electricians.

**Headline**: "Magic Baloney"
**Subheadline**: "You'll love the way we hated the Magic Stat thermostat until an amazing thing happened."

**Winning Angle**: Easy self-installation (manufacturers hadn't even realized this was the key benefit)

### Writing Style for Technical Products

- Use terms people already know
- Explain complexity simply
- Focus on user benefits, not specs
- Create "dreamy optimism of improved life through [product]"

### Home Improvement Application

For technical aspects (permits, materials, etc.):

| Technical Feature | Sugarman-Style Benefit |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| "We handle permit acquisition" | "You won't spend a single hour at city hall" |
| "Commercial-grade materials" | "The same flooring they use in restaurants that see 500 people a day" |
| "Licensed contractors" | "If something goes wrong, you're protected. Period." |

## Storytelling Technique

### Core Principle (Axiom 17)

> "Telling a story can effectively sell your product, create the environment or get the reader well into your copy as you create an emotional bonding with your prospect."

### Story Structure in Ads

1. **Open with personal experience** or friend's story
2. **Establish authenticity** ("I am about to tell you a true story")
3. **Take reader on journey of discovery**
4. **Write like conversation among friends**
5. **Let product emerge naturally from narrative**

### Customer Perspective Approach

Sugarman started most ads from customer's viewpoint:
- Their initial thoughts and expectations
- What surprised them (positively)
- Their transformation/result

### Home Improvement Application

**Instead of**:
> "Prep It List It Sell It Services provides comprehensive home preparation for sellers in Santa Clarita Valley."

**Sugarman-Style**:
> "Maria called on a Tuesday. Her realtor had just told her something she didn't want to hear.
>
> 'Your kitchen is costing you $30,000.'
>
> Not the appliances. Not a renovation. Just the way it looked to buyers walking through.
>
> But here's what happened next..."

## The BluBlocker Success Formula

### What Made It Work

1. **Education-Based Selling**: Taught about blue light dangers before selling solution
2. **Man-on-the-Street Interviews**: Real reactions from real people
3. **Live Demonstrations**: Showed product working in real-time
4. **Authentic Storytelling**: Personal discovery narrative
5. **Infomercial Innovation**: Three-act structure (Entertainment, Selling, Closing)

### Results

- 100,000 pairs sold in first month of infomercial
- 300,000 pairs/month at peak
- 20+ million total pairs sold
- Pizza Hut partnership: 10 million units

### Application Lessons

For home services:
- **Show before/after** (like BluBlocker clarity demonstration)
- **Feature real client reactions** (testimonial style)
- **Educate on the problem** before presenting solution
- **Make the invisible visible** (what buyers see that sellers miss)

## Long-Form Copy Principles

### When to Use Long Copy

> "Copy should be long enough to cause the reader to take the action you request."

### Structure for Long-Form

1. **Pre-headline** (optional teaser)
2. **Headline** (attention-grabber)
3. **Deck copy** (bridge to body)
4. **Opening** (slippery slide begins)
5. **4+ Subheadings** (break up pace, tell story when skimmed)
6. **Body copy** (education + persuasion)
7. **Offer**
8. **Close**

### Making Long Copy Readable

- Short paragraphs
- Subheadings every few paragraphs
- Seeds of curiosity throughout
- Rhythm and flow (read aloud)
- Personal, conversational tone

## Practical Formulas

### The "Yes" Ladder

Make readers agree with small truths to build momentum:
1. State obvious truth reader will nod at
2. State another agreeable point
3. Continue building agreement
4. Introduce your solution while reader is in "yes" mode

### The Honesty Formula

1. Identify obvious flaw or concern
2. Acknowledge it directly and first
3. Explain why it's not actually a problem
4. Turn weakness into strength

### The Curiosity Gap Formula

1. Hint at interesting information
2. Withhold the key detail
3. Promise revelation if they keep reading
4. Deliver on promise (eventually)

### The Involvement Formula

1. Ask reader to imagine or picture something
2. Place them in the scenario mentally
3. Let them feel ownership before purchase
4. Make purchase feel like natural next step

## Application Checklist for Home Improvement Copy

### Before Writing
- [ ] Research: Know the service better than anyone
- [ ] Find the unexpected angle (what do homeowners not realize?)
- [ ] Identify the "cure" you're selling (not prevention)
- [ ] Collect specific details and numbers

### Opening
- [ ] First sentence is SHORT (5 words or fewer ideal)
- [ ] Opens curiosity loop
- [ ] Feels personal and conversational
- [ ] Creates appropriate buying environment

### Body
- [ ] Slippery slide: each sentence compels the next
- [ ] Seeds of curiosity at paragraph ends
- [ ] Objections addressed proactively
- [ ] Specificity over vagueness
- [ ] Story elements (real scenarios, outcomes)

### Psychological Triggers Used
- [ ] Honesty/Credibility established
- [ ] Value proven with specifics
- [ ] Urgency (appropriate to context)
- [ ] Social proof/belonging
- [ ] Involvement/ownership created

### Technical Content
- [ ] Complex made simple
- [ ] Benefits over features
- [ ] Terms readers already know
- [ ] "Dreamy optimism" about outcomes

### Close
- [ ] Purchase justified
- [ ] Action clearly stated
- [ ] Easy next step
- [ ] Momentum maintained to the end
