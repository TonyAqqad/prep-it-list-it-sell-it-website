# Matt Furey: Email Copywriting Master

## Overview

Matt Furey is widely recognized as one of the greatest email copywriters in direct response marketing. Called the "world's greatest email copywriter" by marketing legends including Dan Kennedy, Mark Ford, Ben Settle, and Harlan Kilstein, Furey built a multi-million dollar empire selling fitness products (Combat Conditioning, Combat Abs) and later copywriting courses---almost entirely through email marketing.

**Background:**
- 1985 NCAA II national wrestling champion
- 1997 Shuai-Chiao kung fu world champion (90kg)
- President of The Psycho-Cybernetics Foundation
- Author of international bestseller "Combat Conditioning"
- Known as the "Zen Master of the Internet"

## Core Philosophy: Zero Resistance Writing

Furey's foundational approach is called **Zero Resistance Writing**---writing without mental blocks, where words pour out like "water cascading over Niagara Falls."

### Key Principles

1. **It's the emotional state, not just the words**: "It isn't the words as much as WHO wrote the words and the emotional state he was in when he wrote them."

2. **Speed equals money**: As Dan Kennedy says, "Money is attracted to speed." Furey writes 400-600 word emails in 10-12 minutes.

3. **No editing philosophy**: He doesn't re-read, doesn't edit, just writes and hits send.

4. **Flow state preparation**: Uses "Theatre of the Mind" (a Psycho-Cybernetics visualization technique) for approximately 15 minutes before writing to enter the right mental state.

### The 4 Mindset Keys (from his 11-point framework)

The first four keys focus on getting into:
- The right frame of mind
- The right mental state
- The right emotional space
- A state where words flow effortlessly onto the screen

This approach can reduce editing by up to 80%.

## Daily Email Strategy

### The Core Commitment

Furey sends emails **every single day**. While most marketers worry about annoying their audience, Furey proves that:

> "Frequency builds familiarity---and familiarity builds trust."

### Why Daily Works

- More emails = more money (he's proven this repeatedly)
- Consistent contact keeps you top-of-mind
- No complex funnels or ad strategies needed
- Generates consistent income purely through email

### The "Lean List" Philosophy

Furey embraces unsubscribes rather than fearing them:

> "He'd rather build a list of die-hard fans than a huge, lukewarm audience."

This mindset keeps his list:
- Lean
- Loyal
- Highly responsive

**Application for Home Improvement/Listing Prep:**
Send daily or near-daily emails with helpful tips, local stories, or seasonal reminders. Let uncommitted subscribers leave---the remaining list will be more engaged and ready to hire.

## Storytelling Techniques

### The Story-First Approach

Furey never leads with a sales pitch. His formula:

1. **Tell an engaging story** (often personal, from daily life)
2. **Weave in valuable content**
3. **Subtly transition to the sales pitch**
4. **Strengthen with a P.S.**

### The Master Transition

> "Matt's a master of taking what happened in his daily life and what's in the news, then transitioning into the product he is selling. The story seems so relevant."

**Key insight**: The offer emerges as the "natural solution," making readers feel like buying was their own idea.

### Story Grounding Technique

Start emails with **specific time and place**:
- "Last Thursday at the gym..."
- "Sitting in my favorite coffee shop..."
- "Yesterday morning, as I walked into the hardware store..."

This creates immediate curiosity and makes content feel personal.

**Application for Home Improvement:**
- "Last Tuesday, I walked into a listing in Valencia that made me stop cold..."
- "Standing in the garage of a 1990s home yesterday..."
- "The realtor called me at 7am, panic in her voice..."

## Subject Line Mastery

### The Open Loop Technique

Furey's subject lines create **open loops**---starting a story without finishing it. The brain craves closure.

### Classic Examples

| Subject Line | Why It Works |
|--------------|--------------|
| "The strangest thing happened at the seminar..." | Vague intrigue, forces reader to wonder "what?" |
| "Why I almost quit yesterday..." | Personal, emotional, creates tension |

### Subject Line Formula

1. Think of a story or point you want to make
2. Pick a small, intriguing detail
3. Don't give it all away
4. Make readers feel they're missing something if they don't open

### Subject Line Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake #1**: Always beginning with the reader's first name
- Using it occasionally works, but overuse becomes a weakness
- "A strength over-extended becomes a weakness"

**Mistake #2**: Listing contents at the top
- Instead, suck readers into your universe with riveting copy

**Application for Home Improvement:**
- "What the inspector found behind the water heater..."
- "The one repair that killed the sale..."
- "Why I walked away from a $40,000 project..."

## The "Cowboy" Writing Style

### Deliberate Unconventionality

A graphic artist once told Furey he "writes like a cowboy." He embraced it, creating a distinctive style featuring:

- **Deliberate misspellings**: "world" becomes "whirld," "guru" becomes "goo-roo"
- **Invented words and phrases**
- **Improper grammar used purposefully**
- **Slang and colloquialisms**

### Why It Works

Despite the unconventional approach, Furey gets "raving reviews from highly-educated and decorated coaches, doctors, PhDs, military officers, lawyers."

**The principle**: Authenticity and personality trump polish.

### Multiple Voices

Furey embraces having "two, three, five, or even two-hundred and five voices" in his writing. This allows:
- Fresh perspectives in each email
- Unpredictability that keeps readers engaged
- Personality that builds connection

**Application for Home Improvement:**
Write like you talk on job sites. If you'd say "that old bathroom was a hot mess," write that---not "the bathroom required significant updating."

## Challenging Industry Norms

### The Anti-Professional Stance

One of Furey's most polarizing positions: rejecting "professionalism" in email marketing.

> "Sounding too professional kills engagement because it feels robotic and inauthentic."

### How to Challenge Norms

1. Identify common beliefs in your field
2. Question whether they're actually true
3. Present your contrarian viewpoint boldly
4. Back it up with experience and results

**Application for Home Improvement:**
- Challenge the "get 3 quotes" wisdom
- Question the HGTV renovation mentality
- Call out over-improvement for the neighborhood
- Be honest about what repairs actually matter for selling

## Email Structure & Formatting

### Optimal Length

**400-600 words** per email---enough to tell a story and make an offer, short enough to write quickly (10-12 minutes).

### Conversational Tone

Write as if talking to a friend:
- Simple, accessible vocabulary
- Rhetorical questions
- Inject personality and humor
- Avoid the "salesy" feel

### Scannable Formatting

For busy readers who skim:
- Use bullet points and bold highlights
- Keep paragraphs short
- Create visual hierarchy guiding to key points and CTAs

### The Q&A Style

Format emails as direct questions and conversational answers:
- Feels spontaneous
- Addresses reader concerns directly
- Naturally transitions into pitches without pressure

## Calls-to-Action

### Never Use Generic CTAs

Avoid: "Click Here"

Instead, craft CTAs that emphasize benefits:
- "Start transforming your business today"
- "Join thousands who've already succeeded"
- "Get your free listing prep checklist now"

### Scarcity and Urgency (Without Being Pushy)

Furey creates urgency naturally through:
- Limited-time offers
- Exclusive bonuses
- Limited availability
- Decision deadlines

**The key**: Make it feel real, not manufactured.

## The "Infotaining" Approach

### Information + Entertainment

Blend teaching with entertainment through:
- Humor and wit
- Vivid imagery
- Unexpected connections
- Stories that teach

### Use What's Popular

Tie messages to:
- Trending topics
- Viral stories
- Current news
- Local events

This increases relevance and engagement because readers already pay attention to these subjects.

**Application for Home Improvement:**
- Connect a viral home renovation fail to your services
- Reference a local news story about the housing market
- Tie seasonal events to home maintenance

## Key Formulas & Templates

### The Daily Story-to-Sale Formula

```
1. Open with specific time/place
2. Tell an engaging personal story
3. Extract a lesson or insight
4. Connect to reader's situation
5. Transition to offer as natural solution
6. Clear CTA with benefit focus
7. P.S. that reinforces or adds urgency
```

### The Open Loop Subject Line Formula

```
[Emotion/Action word] + [Vague reference to situation] + [Ellipsis or question]

Examples:
- "What happened when..."
- "The mistake that almost..."
- "Why I never..."
- "The strangest thing about..."
```

### The Contrast Technique

Place opposing ideas side-by-side:
- Conventional approach vs. your unconventional method
- What everyone says vs. what you know works
- Common mistake vs. better alternative

### Story Sources

- Job site incidents and discoveries
- Client transformations (before/after)
- Realtor feedback and success stories
- Neighborhood observations
- Seasonal challenges
- Common homeowner mistakes

### Subject Line Ideas

### Daily Email Topics

| Day | Topic Type | Example |
|-----|------------|---------|
| Monday | Project story | "What I found under the deck boards..." |
| Tuesday | Quick tip | "The 5-minute check every seller forgets" |
